Andre Montrichard

Fahrenheit 451 is a popular science fiction dystopian novel written by american author Ray Bradbury in 1953, the book is famous for its stance on books being superior to electronic media and its anti censorship themes. The central plot of the book follows protagonist Guy Montag who is a fireman in a futuristic version of the world, in Guy’s world people don’t read books, partake in meaningful conversations, or appreciate nature. Additionally the firemen of this world don’t put out fires, instead they start them to burn any books that are found as the government deems them to be propaganda that encourages independent thought. Montag one day finds himself disconnected from his daily life and decides to read one of the books that he stole from a burn site. This causes Montag to seek out more knowledge but in the process lose everything that he once had in his life, when almost everyone in his life turns on him, leaving Montag to go on the run from the authorities. This text was chosen to be the subject of this project as it is an incredibly influential text with its stance on censorship, predictive technological advancements and warnings on the dangers of mass media. The visual models shown within this project will illustrate the prevalence of the main types of media presented within the text as well as the prevalence of several characters in the supporting cast.

Within the text there are many forms of media such as television, seashell radios, parlor walls and books. The dominant form is of course television, but other forms like seashell radios which are radios that fit into the listeners ear and parlor walls which is an interactive television that's the size of a wall in a home are all prominent in day to day lives of people living in guys time. The main three that will be focused on in the visualization are books, television, and seashell radios(shortened to seashell because the word is never used outside that context).
